Travel to Maryland

Arriving: If you are flying in for the wedding, Washington, DC is served by three airports: Baltimore (BWI), Washington Dulles (IAD), and Washington Reagan (DCA). BWI is closest to the wedding venue.
Travel via train or bus are also good options if you live on the East Coast.


Getting around: Traveling within Maryland is easiest with a car, so do consider renting one! Rideshare apps are plentiful, too, so you can let others do the driving. Lyft and Uber are the big ones. Feeling brave and want the wind in your hair? Snag a scooter or a bikeshare! Please be safe. K. Sarah has a love/hate relationship with Metro, but it certainly gets you around. You'll need to purchase a SmarTrip card.


Pro Tips:

  • If you're exploring downtown DC, especially the National Mall, we highly recommend NOT DRIVING because parking is scarce and expensive AND there's tons of traffic. Yay tourists! Take public transit (stand right walk left!) or rideshare.
  • If you're staying close to the hotel and wedding venue, you'll either want to rent a car or use a rideshare app.
  • Before riding Metro, please check WMATA for alerts regarding weekend service. It changes all the time :)
  • To go on an adventure, use the Transit app - it has everything from Metro and buses to commuter rail and scooters.
